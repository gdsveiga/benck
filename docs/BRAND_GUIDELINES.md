## Brand Overview

**Benck** is a technology company that specializes in creating fully customized CRM and ERP solutions for small and medium-sized businesses. Unlike generic, one-size-fits-all software, Benck builds applications tailored to each client's unique workflows, processes, and needs.

**What We Do:**
- Custom CRM development
- Custom ERP solutions
- Business process automation
- Internal workflow optimization
- Ongoing support and iteration

**Our Promise:**
We don't force your business to adapt to software‚Äîwe build software that adapts to your business.

## Brand Story

The name **Benck** has a personal origin. It comes from the names of the founders' two dogs: **Zack** and **Bento**. This might seem unconventional for a tech company, but it reflects something important about who we are: we're real people who care about the things (and beings) that matter to us.

This personal touch extends to how we work with clients. We're not a faceless corporation. We're a team that takes the time to understand your business, your challenges, and your goals‚Äîjust like we take the time to understand what makes Zack and Bento happy.

The period in **Benck.** represents completeness and confidence. It's a statement, not a question. When you work with Benck, you get a complete solution.

## Brand Voice & Tone

### Voice Characteristics

Our brand voice is consistent across all communications:

| Characteristic | What It Means | Example |
|----------------|---------------|---------|
| **Professional** | We know our craft and communicate with expertise | "Our solutions integrate seamlessly with your existing infrastructure" |
| **Friendly** | We're approachable and human, never cold or corporate | "Let's figure this out together" |
| **Innovative** | We embrace modern solutions and forward-thinking approaches | "We build for where your business is going, not just where it is" |
| **Clear** | We avoid jargon and explain things simply | "Your data, organized and accessible" instead of "Optimized data architecture paradigms" |
| **Confident** | We stand behind our work without being arrogant | "We'll build exactly what you need" |

### Tone Adjustments by Context

| Context | Tone Adjustment |
|---------|-----------------|
| Website copy | Confident, inspiring, benefit-focused |
| Social media | Friendly, conversational, engaging |
| Client proposals | Professional, thorough, reassuring |
| Support communications | Warm, patient, solution-oriented |
| Technical documentation | Clear, precise, helpful |

### Writing Guidelines

**Do:**
- Use active voice ("We build solutions" not "Solutions are built by us")
- Address the reader directly ("your business," "you'll see")
- Keep sentences concise
- Lead with benefits, follow with features
- Use real-world examples

**Don't:**
- Use excessive technical jargon
- Sound robotic or overly formal
- Make promises you can't keep
- Use buzzwords without substance
- Talk down to the reader

## Typography

### Primary Typeface: Inter

**Inter** is our primary typeface. It's modern, highly legible, and works excellently across digital and print applications. It's also free and widely available.

**Alternative:** If Inter is unavailable, use **Poppins** or **Montserrat**.

### Type Hierarchy

| Element | Font | Weight | Size (Web) |
|---------|------|--------|------------|
| H1 - Headlines | Inter | Bold (700) | 48-64px |
| H2 - Section Headers | Inter | Bold (700) | 32-40px |
| H3 - Subsections | Inter | SemiBold (600) | 24-28px |
| Body Text | Inter | Regular (400) | 16-18px |
| Captions/Small | Inter | Regular (400) | 14px |
| Buttons/CTAs | Inter | SemiBold (600) | 16px |

### Typography Guidelines

- Use **bold weights** for headlines to create impact
- Maintain comfortable line heights (1.5 for body text)
- Left-align body text; center headlines sparingly
- Use sentence case for headlines ("Build your perfect CRM")
- Avoid all caps except for small labels or buttons

## Brand Do's and Don'ts

### Do's ‚úÖ

- **Do** maintain consistency across all touchpoints
- **Do** use the approved color palette
- **Do** keep messaging client-focused (benefits over features)
- **Do** be approachable and human
- **Do** back claims with specifics
- **Do** use high-quality images and graphics
- **Do** leave plenty of white space in designs
- **Do** proofread everything before publishing
- **Do** consider accessibility in all designs

### Don'ts ‚ùå

- **Don't** use colors outside the approved palette
- **Don't** distort or modify the logo
- **Don't** use overly technical jargon
- **Don't** make promises you can't deliver
- **Don't** use low-quality or pixelated images
- **Don't** clutter designs with too many elements
- **Don't** use a casual tone in formal proposals
- **Don't** copy competitor messaging or positioning
- **Don't** forget the period in "Benck."
